Stroke Red Flag Symptoms Guidance

Overview
Stroke is a neurological emergency caused by interruption of blood flow to the brain. Rapid identification and treatment are critical to reduce permanent neurological damage.

Key Stroke Warning Signs

The FAST mnemonic is commonly used:

F – Facial drooping
A – Arm weakness
S – Speech difficulty
T – Time to call emergency services

Additional Neurological Symptoms

Possible stroke symptoms include:

* Sudden weakness or numbness on one side of the body
* Sudden confusion
* Difficulty speaking or understanding speech
* Sudden vision loss in one or both eyes
* Severe sudden headache with no known cause
* Loss of balance or coordination
* Difficulty walking

Headache and Stroke

A sudden severe headache may indicate:

* Hemorrhagic stroke
* Subarachnoid hemorrhage

This type of headache is often described as:

"the worst headache of my life"

Emergency Response

If stroke symptoms are suspected:

* Immediate emergency medical evaluation is required
* Brain imaging (CT or MRI) should be performed urgently
* Thrombolytic therapy may be considered within appropriate time windows

Clinical Recommendation

Any patient presenting with acute neurological deficits should be treated as a possible stroke until proven otherwise.
