Tension-Type Headache Clinical Guidance

Overview
Tension-type headache is the most common primary headache disorder. It is typically characterized by mild to moderate bilateral head pain with a pressing or tightening sensation.

Common Clinical Features

Typical symptoms include:

* Bilateral head pain
* Pressing or tightening sensation
* Mild to moderate intensity
* Not aggravated by physical activity
* No significant nausea or vomiting

Patients may report:

* Sensation of tight band around head
* Neck and shoulder muscle tightness
* Scalp tenderness

Duration

Tension-type headaches may last:

* 30 minutes to several hours
* Occasionally several days

Diagnostic Criteria

Diagnosis is suggested when headaches have:

* Bilateral location
* Pressing or tightening quality
* Mild to moderate intensity
* No aggravation by routine activity

And both of the following:

* No nausea or vomiting
* No more than one of photophobia or phonophobia

Triggers and Risk Factors

Common triggers include:

* Emotional stress
* Poor posture
* Prolonged screen use
* Sleep deprivation
* Muscle tension

Evaluation

Routine imaging is not required unless red flag symptoms are present, including:

* Neurological deficits
* Sudden severe onset
* Progressive worsening headaches
* Systemic symptoms (fever, weight loss)

Acute Treatment

First-line therapy includes:

* Acetaminophen
* Ibuprofen
* Naproxen

Non-pharmacologic treatments include:

* Rest
* Hydration
* Stretching
* Stress reduction techniques

Preventive Therapy

For frequent tension headaches:

* Amitriptyline may be considered
* Behavioral therapy
* Stress management programs
* Physical therapy

Lifestyle Recommendations

Patients should be advised to:

* Maintain proper posture
* Take regular breaks from screen use
* Practice relaxation techniques
* Ensure adequate sleep
