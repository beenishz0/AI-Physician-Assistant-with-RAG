Acute Sinusitis Clinical Guidance

Overview
Acute sinusitis (acute rhinosinusitis) is inflammation of the paranasal sinuses typically caused by viral or bacterial infection. It commonly presents with facial pressure and headache.

Common Symptoms

Patients may present with:

* Facial pressure or pain
* Headache localized to forehead or cheeks
* Nasal congestion
* Thick nasal discharge
* Reduced sense of smell
* Tooth pain (upper teeth)
* Fever

Pain Pattern

Sinus-related headaches often:

* Worsen when bending forward
* Are associated with nasal congestion
* Occur with facial tenderness over sinus areas

Physical Findings

Typical exam findings include:

* Tenderness over frontal or maxillary sinuses
* Purulent nasal discharge
* Nasal mucosal swelling

Diagnostic Considerations

Acute sinusitis is usually diagnosed clinically.

Bacterial sinusitis should be suspected when symptoms:

* Persist longer than 10 days
* Worsen after initial improvement
* Are associated with high fever and purulent discharge

Treatment

Initial management includes:

* Nasal saline irrigation
* Intranasal corticosteroids
* Decongestants
* Analgesics

Antibiotics may be considered when bacterial infection is suspected.

Common antibiotic options:

* Amoxicillin-clavulanate
* Doxycycline in penicillin-allergic patients

Referral

Patients with recurrent or chronic sinusitis should be referred to an ENT specialist.
