Meningitis Red Flag Guidance

Overview
Meningitis is inflammation of the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. It can be caused by bacterial, viral, or fungal infections.

Bacterial meningitis is a life-threatening medical emergency requiring immediate treatment.

Common Symptoms

Patients with meningitis may present with:

* Severe headache
* Fever
* Neck stiffness
* Sensitivity to light (photophobia)
* Nausea and vomiting
* Altered mental status

Classic Meningitis Triad

The classic triad includes:

* Fever
* Neck stiffness
* Altered mental status

However, not all patients present with all three symptoms.

Additional Findings

Possible additional signs include:

* Confusion
* Difficulty concentrating
* Seizures
* Sensitivity to light
* Skin rash (in meningococcal infection)

Physical Examination

Clinicians may observe:

* Neck stiffness
* Positive Kernig sign
* Positive Brudzinski sign

Diagnostic Evaluation

Suspected meningitis requires urgent medical evaluation.

Diagnostic tests include:

* Blood cultures
* Lumbar puncture
* Cerebrospinal fluid analysis
* Brain imaging if indicated

Treatment

Suspected bacterial meningitis requires:

* Immediate intravenous antibiotics
* Possible corticosteroids
* Hospital admission

Clinical Recommendation

Patients presenting with fever, severe headache, and neck stiffness should be evaluated urgently for possible meningitis.
